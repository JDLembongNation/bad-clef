Blood Shadows

A flurry of red in a pitch of darkness

The red flowers laid in an unorderly manner procure a novel representation of human life. If we imagine the flowers as humans, we can see in detail the expression of the human need for accompaniment. Not one flower is left alone and encapsulated in darkness. Flowers grow together in bunches and stay close to other groups of flowers. Some are in greater groups than others, though when we view the photo as a whole, we can see the prevalent theme. Only we can light up the darkness, and it is in groups where we shine the brightest.

This photo also presents another interpretation. Where we usually see flowers in bunches, we don't see individuality in each flower. However, in this photo, each flower's outline can be discerned. The black background contrasts well with the blood-red flower. The original photo had summer leaves filling the picture with green and making all flowers insignificant. Now with most of the bright green colors gone into darkness, we are left with the flowers that stand out from the crowd. Suggesting that when we're in a crowd eccentric people fall victim to insignificance. However, once normality plunges, those that stay eccentric will prevail above darkness.
